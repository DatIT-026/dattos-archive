Talking About MacBook and MacOS: Why I Hate It

Let’s go back to 2001, when Apple introduced MacOS with big promises of a shiny interface that was “so easy that anyone could use it.” Then in 2006, the MacBook arrived, and everyone thought it would rule the laptop world. But reality hit differently. After spending some time with it, I eventually had to say goodbye for a few reasons: 👇

I have to admit, MacOS on a MacBook is undeniably smooth and well-optimized. However, there are certain things that make the experience quite… frustrating. This post is just my personal take on why this duo failed to meet my expectations, and why you shouldn’t treat them like some kind of “holy grail” of technology.

Lack of Customization

MacOS follows a “take-it-or-leave-it” design philosophy, super consistent, but it suffocates personal creativity. If you’ve used Windows or are a Linux enthusiast (like me, currently on Fedora and previously on NixOS) who loves tweaking everything, MacOS will be a total shock. It only lets you adjust a few basic things for fun. Want to change folder colors or mess with system icons? It’s nearly impossible, a true pain for “power users”!

Limited Compatibility with Third-Party Software

MacOS often lacks compatibility with non-Apple applications. If you need specialized software, you might find it’s unavailable on MacOS, as developers typically prioritize Windows due to its larger market share. Virtualization exists as an option, but it serves more as a workaround than a true solution. This is one factor contributing to the lack of customization I mentioned earlier.

Gaming Experience

Are you a gamer or do you want to play some AAA games to de-stress? Sorry to say, but MacBook is definitely not on your side. Apple doesn’t seem to care about gaming at all, which results in a very poor game library. The lack of DirectX support, the “soul” of Windows games, makes even the ported games struggle to run. As for the Mac App Store, there is nothing much to say except that it’s boring.

So, if you are not the “all work and no play” type, take a look at brands like Acer, Asus, or Lenovo. You get good specs and great gaming without hurting your wallet as much as buying a MacBook.

Proprietary Hardware and Overpriced

Indeed! I’d have to say that MacBook is delusional with pricing. From my perspective, brands like Dell, Acer, and Lenovo are not only the alternative options to save money if you’re on a budget but also can still handle all your office tasks effectively.

One major drawback of MacOS is its requirement for Apple hardware. Although MacBooks and iMacs are beautiful, they are expensive. Users must buy a Mac to access MacOS, with no option to build a more affordable custom system like with PCs.

And who the heck said that 8GB of RAM on MacOS is the same as 16GB on a Laptop Windows?? Foolish!

Incompatibility with Industry Standards

If you work in a field where Windows is a “king”, using MacOS is like trying to speak a different language in your own home. Microsoft Office on Mac always feels like a “lite” version, missing many features that are standard on Windows. This makes collaborating with colleagues or clients a total headache and very clunky.

6. Hardware Upgrading? Apple says no!

Apple’s decision to use proprietary components limits user flexibility. Since RAM and storage are often soldered in, upgrading is either difficult or impossible, leaving you with a fixed configuration and few options for increased power later.

After three years, let’s see if the person who said that 8GB Mac RAM is the same as 16GB Windows RAM could say that again :))

Conclusion

The MacBook has excellent build quality, and MacOS offers stable performance and strong privacy, which is why it has such a loyal fanbase. However, the high cost and the limitations mentioned above remain massive hurdles. If you’re an Apple “die-hard” and ready to ignore all the flaws - especially that stinging price tag - then go for it. If not, there are plenty of “other lands” out there that might suit you better.

Even though Apple is still the “boss” thanks to top-tier quality, if they don’t fix these weaknesses, even loyal customers might eventually “pull a U-turn” because of the cost!
